# Overview and general principles

In order for you to know where you are going, we will have a little tour on the project and the main idea of this box

![working-principle-schema](../assets/dotpi-box/overview/working-principle-schema.png)

The box is made of <span style="color:red">four wooden fingered joints faces (face 2 to 5)</span>, <span style="color:steelblue">one plastic fingered joined face (face 1)</span> on which the speaker is fixed and <span style="color:yellow">one wooden flat joined mobile part (part 6)</span> on which the pi module is fixed.

Because a speaker cabinet must (in general) be a tight area, and because a Raspberry Pi and Amp module tend to release heat and must be accessible, the two systems are separated by a <span style="color:purple"> plastic separation</span> that also acts as a support for external ports (power supply) and as a guide when mounting the box.

The materials used for each part are :

- <span style="color:red"> Faces 2, 3, 4, 5</span> and <span style="color:yellow"> face 6</span> : 5mm plywood
- <span style="color:steelblue">Face 1</span> : PAHT-CF (PA12 and carbon fiber composite)
- <span style="color:purple">Separation</span> : regular PLA

The main idea will be to laser cut all the wooden part, 3D print the plastic, and glue everything together in the right order. :wink:
